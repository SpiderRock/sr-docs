FLASH Auctions

Our FLASH auction mechanism allows initiators to interact with a network of electronic liquidity providers in an open competitive process. FLASH auctions can be inititated either from EXS or third party algorithms or from individual initiators via the SpiderRock TradeTool. The handling of the order before and after a FLASH auction typically depends on the configuration of the EXS or third party algo that initiated the order. In some cases the order will be returned to the originating party unfilled or partially filled. In other configurations the order will be forwarded to an exchange for handling within an exchange order book. In addition, FLASH auctions can be used as part of a progression style algo (eg. a TWAP algo) that works through a sequence of order handling steps over time.

FLASH auctions can be used to try and find competitive price improvement ahead of routing to an exchange order book. They can also be used to attempt to find liquidity at the NBBO price level as an alternative to paying a high exchange taker fee. And they can be used to try and find additional size above what is offered at NBBO price levels.

Flash Auction Initiator Parameters​

Series or Spread​

The auction initiator must supply an equity option series or spread. This can be either a listed option series, and exchange eligible listed option spread, or a valid FLEX option series or spread. Spreads can contain up to SIX option legs and may contain and equity leg.

Order Side​

The auction initiator must supply an order side (BUY or SELL) if for an individual option series or a spread side (BUY/REG, SELL/INV) if for an option spread. Note that the initiator can choose the spread leg package including the leg sides. As a result, if an initiator is starting an auction with their side visible they would typically chose to start with their side as BUY/REG but are not required to do so. This cannot be changed after the auction starts.

Limit Price​

The auction initiator must supply either a static or formula based limit price that is at least mid market or better relative to either exact OPRA NBBO levels or SpiderRock fair price (surface price) levels at the start of the auction process.

Side/Price Visibility​

The auction initiator must chose to whether to reveal their side and price, their side only, or neither.

If the initiator chooses to reveal their side at the start of an auction for a spread then the resulting auction notices will be sent to potential responders with the leg package as defined by the initiator. Responders are free to respond to the initiator's leg package but with the opposite order side (eg, if the initiator is BUY/REG the responder would be SELL/INV). Alternatively, responders may respond with the same order side as the initator (eg. BUY/REG vs BUY/REG) but with an inverted order leg package (SELL .switch. BUY) and in inverted limit price sign.

If the initiator chooses to NOT reveal their side at the start of an auction for a spread then the resulting auction notices will be sent to potential responders with the leg package arranged to make the first leg side a BUY regardless of whether or not the initiator arranged the legs that way. As a result, it may not be possible for responders to immediately or reliably determine which side the initiator is acting from.

Counterparties​

The initiator can choose which counterparties receive auction notices. This is done by either specifying a list of named counterparties (either to include or exclude) or by electing to send notices to the entire SpiderConnect network or some combination.

Identity Disclosure​

The initiator can choose to identify themselves to some or all of their notice counterparties. If an initiator chooses to identify themselves to a counterparty then that counterparty will be identified to the initiator if/when they respond. Similarly if an initiator does NOT identify themselves to a responder then the identity of that responder will NOT be revealed to the initiator if/when they respond.

Note that in some cases initiators who are broker dealers and that identify themselves to a responder may also choose to receive a commission (specified on the FLASH auction notice) from the responder should the initiator and responder trade with each other.

Flash Auction Responder Parameters​

Series or Spread​

Auction responders must respond with a specific (unique) auction notice ID that idetifies an individual auction. If a response to a specific auction does not include an option series or spread the response will be deemed a response to the noticed option series or option spread package. If for a spread the response will be deemed to be a response with the legs oriented as they were in the auction notice. If the initiator has revealed a side the responder order side must be the opposite that of the initiator (BUY vs SELL or BUY/REG vs SELL/INV). Otherwise the responder can respond on either side (or both). If the initiator subsequently reveals a side then all incompatible (same direction) responses will be immediately cancelled with nothing done.

Responders may, if they choose, respond to an auction notice ID by supplying the auction notice ID AND a corresponding option series or spread. In this case the option series or spread must be compatible with the initiator series or spread. Note that when responding to spreads it is possible for the responder to use the same order side as the initiator (eg. BUY/REG vs BUY/REG) but the opposite leg sides and limit price sign. This type of response will be accepted as valid and automatically inverted within SpiderRock Connect to align with other responses.

Order Side​

Auction responders must supply an order side (BUY or SELL) if for an individual option series or a spread side (BUY/REG, SELL/INV) if for a spread.

Limit Price​

Auction responders must supply either a static or formula based limit price. This limit price can be changed at any time during the auction and will become effective in the next trial match if changed.

Best Price Offset​

Responders may enter a Best Price Offset. If supplied, this Best Price Offset will only be used to join other responders. Also, if a presumtive match occurs within SpiderRock Connect and is subject to an price improvement auction on exchange then any remaining Best Price Offset room will be forwarded to the exchange for consideration in the on-exchange secondary auction. If more than one responder is part of the presumtive match then the least common remaining Best Price Offset value will be forwarded to the exchange for consideration.

Flash Auction Notices​

At the start of a flash auction SpiderRock Connect will send auction notice to all eligible responders. Each individual responder notice will respect the initiator counterparty and identity disclosure parameters.

Responder may receive notices (AuctionNotice messages) via SpiderRock MLink servers (electronic notices).

In addition, SpiderRock will check all pre-registered auction auto-responder instructions for potential compatibility and will automatically generate an auction response if compatible.

Flash Auction Mechanics​

Flash Trial Match​

A FLASH auction trial match will be run 100ms after the start of the auction.

At the start of this trial match SpiderRock Connect will determine a set of valid auction responses that are able to fully execute at that time.

Next SpiderRock Connect will identify a relevent stock NBBO level, resolve all formula based limits (if any), and round initiator and responder limits to an appropriate potential match price level. Note that individual orders can either round exact (99/1) or round fuzzy (90/10) in the direction of making the order more conservative. This choice is established by user preference at the trading account level.

In most cases all potential match price levels will be to 2 decimal places. In the special case of a spread that contains an stock leg with a leg size not equal to 100 the initiator's limit price will be rounded (50/50) to 4 decimal places and all potential match price levels will be to 4 decimal places spaced $0.01 appart with the 3rd and 4th decimal place being the 3rd and 4th decimal place of the initiator's rounded limit price.

During the process of forming price levels some specific rules apply.

• No responder price level can be equal to or through the opposing OPRA NBBO level. For example if NBBO is 1.12 - 1.17 for a single series and a responder attempts to SELL at 1.08 that response limit will be adjusted to 1.13 (one penny inside the NBBO BID). For a spread order the bounding NBBO will be the NBBO level from the individual leg markets (options and stock).

• No initiator price level can be more than 0.25 through OPRA NBBO levels. For example, if NBBO is 2.54 - 2.75 for a single series and an initiator attempts to BUY at 3.10 their limit price will be adjusted to 3.00. Similarly, for a spread order the bounding NBBO will be the NBBO level from the individual leg markets (options and stock).

• If an initiator has already advertised their limit price all responder limit prices will be stopped at the initiators public price level. For example, if the initiator is advertising a willingness to BUY at 3.42 and a responders sends an order to SELL with a limit of 3.40 then the responders limit will be adjusted to 3.42 to match the initiators already public price. However, if a presumptive match occurs at the 3.42 level the initiator that offered through the initiator level will be deemed to have a best price of 3.40 less any best price offset for the purpose of notifying an exchange of the responders willingness to trade at a better level.

• If a responder has an active best price increment that increment can be activated (used) only if a better competive response is available in the trial match. For a response to be deemed competive it must be for at least 100 cn or 1/4 of the initiator's size.

Next, SpiderRock Connect will form all responses into a response book. This book will be one-sided if the initiator has revealed a price and two-sided otherwise. Note that the response book price levels may be in either 2 or 4 decimal places (depending on the item being auctioned) but will be spaced at least $0.01 apart.

All responder size conditions will be considered and an available size (to the initiator) at each response book price level will be determined.

If it is possible for the initiator to cross (trade with) one more more responders at a price level, subject to all initiator and responder conditions, the best such price level will become the presumptive match price and a presumptive match process will be started. Otherwise, the results of the presumptive match will be advertised to the initiator and all SpiderRock Connect subscribers. This notification will be via electronic message (MLink) and also via the SpiderRock Trade Tool.

Presumptive Match Process​

At the start of presumptive match process a provisional responder side allocation will be determined on a size pro-rata basis and a crossing order forwarded to an exchanged selected by SpiderRock Connect. This crossing order may include a least common residual Best Price Offset value for use competitively on the selected exchange. If some or all of a presumptive match is broken up on exchange then the responder side balance (if any) will be re-allocated in a pro-rata fashion amoungst the participating responders.

If necessary, to satisify exchange or reg NMS requirements for single option series, SpiderRock Connect will sweep away NBBO markets prior to beginning an ISO cross on the selected exchange.

If possible, SpiderRock Connect will utilize QCC style auctions or other non-competitive on-exchange auction processes (eg. cust-cust).

If an exchange rejects a cross attempt SpiderRock Connect may attempt the cross on another exchange immediately. If all exchange crossing possibilities have been exhausted SpiderRock connect will declare the presumptive match broken and all orders will be cancelled with nothing done.

SpiderRock Connect will pay all exchange fees associated with the selected exchange and will arrange for all resulting executions to be delivered via CMTA to the approriate initiator and responder accounts. Responders and/or initiators will pay commissions to or receive commissions from SpiderRock Connect as per their individual initiator and responder agreements.

Flash Auction Notification​

At the conclusion of a flash auction (whether it trades or not) a final AuctionState message is made available to all SpiderRock Connect subscribers via either MLink Servers and/or the SpiderRock TradeTool (if the flash auction was initiated from the tool). This final AuctionNotice contains the resulting responder auction book but does not contain the original initiator side, size, or limit price.
