SRConnect Block and Flash Auctions

SpiderRock Connect Auctions​

SpiderRock offers Block Auctions, which are timed auctions capable of automating upstairs negotiated trades by using a fully electronic price discovery process, and Flash Auctions, which are instantaneous auctions that match initiating interest with upstairs liquidity. These two auction mechanisms utilize our ATS and are seamlessly integrated into our order ticket strategy pulldown. Both auctions offer fully electronic trading capabilities similar to the SpiderRock execution algorithms which have been a core offering of the platform for over a decade. The SpiderRock Connect Network is at the heart of our ATS and is comprised of all SpiderRock clients. This diverse network consists of buyside firms, dealers, and market makers, and is focused on a range of trading strategies.

Block Auctions​

Our innovative Block Auction mechanism removes the need for manual interaction by inviting institutional clients and a wide array of liquidity providers to transact electronically via an efficient trading network. Clients who initiate Block Auctions can access both the SpiderRock Network of liquidity providers as well as existing trading counterparties (which can be implemented via SpiderRock Connect upon request).

Key features of Block Auctions:​

• Timed block auctions have a maximum duration of 15 Sec, 30 Sec, 60 Sec, 2 Min, or 5 Min.


• Auctions are fully transparent with prices of both the initiator and responders visible to all participants.


• All auction participants can manually update their price levels at any point during the auction negotiation process.


• All participants can allow outright orders to be adjusted by a delta, gamma, using a reference price.

• Initiators can customize routing to the SR Network as well as existing trading counterparties.


• Initiators can reveal their identity to existing trading relationships and the SpiderRock Network upon request.


• Initiators can automate the auction process by entering a hidden best price offset that will only be utilized at end of the auction duration.

• Initiators can choose between “All Or None”, “Up To”, and “At Least” size conditions.


• Initiators can cancel their auction at any time, ending the auction for all responders without a fill.

• Responders can choose to utilize a best price offset that improves their public price in an automated manner.


• Responders can cancel their auction response at any point while the auction is still active.

Flash Auctions​

SpiderRock has built its own Flash Auction capability. The SpiderRock Flash Auction immediately matches an initiating order with auto-responding liquidity, while also displaying all responses to aid in price discovery.

SpiderRock's innovative Flash Auction utilizes a two-step process:

In step-one, the initiating client's order (item, size, direction) is sent to a network of auto-responding liquidity providers without disclosing the initiating order's limit price. After receiving the auction notice, the network of liquidity providers respond with both a price and size for the initiating client’s order. Liquidity providers may respond at a single price level or use a price limit with a best price offset.
The best price offset (discussed in more detail in the Block Auction parameters section) gives the liquidity provider flexibility to respond with their desired level as well as their most aggressive level in case the traded level is more aggressive than their desired level.

In step-two, SpiderRock immediately matches the initiating client and liquidity provider's order using both price and size, and exposes the 2-sided order on exchange to be traded. This two-step process requires liquidity providers to respond with price and size before a match is made, resulting in these price and size levels being communicated to the initiating client.

Key features of Flash Auctions:​

• These IOC auctions have a duration of less than 100ms.


• Flash Auctions allow for access to liquidity and price discovery.


• Flash Auction Initiators can choose to reveal their identity.


• SpiderRock provides a transparent process where prices are visible to all auction participants at the conclusion of the IOC auction.


• Flash Auctions have been built into SpiderRock's suite of algorithms offering initiators access to a new pool of liquidity and more flexibility to minimize friction from trading costs.

Allocations on Auction Transactions​

When effecting a presumptive match for either a Flash Auction or a Block Auction, SpiderRock Connect will allocate based on matches according to price and aggregate size. Other applicable size conditions – such as All or None, Up to Quantity, or Quantity or More – may also apply when appropriate. When presumptively matching “pro rata by size” in described auction methods, SpiderRock Connect will first reduce (for allocation purposes only) the size of any response greater than the size of the initiating order.
This quantity is referred to as a “Represented Quantity” in both the below formula and allocation examples. The formula we use to calculate auction allocations is as follows:

Individual Responder Allocation Qty=Qty of Crossed Contracts×(Individual Responder Represented QtyTotal Auction Represented Qty)Individual \space Responder \space Allocation \space Qty = Qty \space of \space Crossed \space Contracts \times (\frac{Individual \space Responder \space Represented \space Qty}{Total \space Auction \space Represented \space Qty})Individual Responder Allocation Qty=Qty of Crossed Contracts×(Total Auction Represented QtyIndividual Responder Represented Qty​)

Here are three examples to illustrate our allocation methodology:

Example 1: Multiple response matches for the full size of an auction.​

Assume an Initiating order of 100 contracts – with a size condition of All or None – and matching responses of (1) 100 contracts, (2) 100 contracts, and (3) 150 contracts. The response for (3) 150 contracts will be treated for allocation purposes as a response for 100 contracts (100 contracts is the individual responder's “Represented Quantity”). Two responses will receive 33 contracts and one response will receive 34 contracts.

Note: The contract rounding is not based on the intial responders’ sizes. Instead, partial contract rounding will be awarded based upon time of receipt, with earlier received responses receiving the rounded-up number of contracts.

Example 2: Multiple response matches for full and partial size of an auction.​

Assume an initiating order of 5,000 contracts and matching responses of (1) 10,000 contracts, (2) 5,000 contracts, (3) 1,000 contracts, (4) 500 contracts, (5) 250 contracts, and (6) 100 contracts. The response for (1) 10,000 contracts will be treated for allocation purposes as a response for 5,000 contracts (5,000 is the individual responder's “Represented Quantity”). In order to determine each responder's allocation we divide each responder’s Represented Quantity by the total of all Represented Quantity for the auction. Assuming that the full 5,000 contracts of the initiator's order is crossed without being broken up, Responder (2) in the below illustration will receive an allocation of:

5,000×(5,00011,850)=2,110 contracts for this auction5,000 \times (\frac{5,000}{11,850}) = 2,110 \space contracts \space for \space this \space auction 5,000×(11,8505,000​)=2,110 contracts for this auction

Example 3: Quantity of crossed contracts is less than the order size.​

In some cases, the quantity of the crossed contracts will be less than the total order size. This can occur when SpiderRock needs to sweep contracts ahead of a cross, some portion of the order has already been traded, or the cross was broken-up on a portion of the quantity. Using the same Order Size, Response Sizes and Represented Quantities from Example 2, but changing the Quantity of Crossed Contracts to 4,000, Responder (2) would receive an allocation of:

4,000×(5,00011,850)=1,6884,000 \times (\frac{5,000}{11,850}) = 1,6884,000×(11,8505,000​)=1,688

Auction Price and Size Restrictions​

Price Restrictions​

Auction participants can enter orders to initiate or respond to a Flash or Block Auction. Please note that those orders will only interact in a given SpiderRock Connect auction if the initiating order is priced at a level that permits a presumptive match against contra-side interest.

Presumptive matching in SRConnect auctions are also subject to applicable regulatory restrictions under Regulation NMS (trade through obligations, prohibitions on locking and crossing markets, etc.) and options exchange order priority. Accordingly, presumptive matches may be delayed or may not occur to the extent that their execution through an options exchange auction would violate a regulatory requirement. As a result, the responder’s side of a presumptive match may be reduced as necessary to satisfy trade through an options exchange’s order priority requirements.

Size Restrictions​

A Flash Auction can be initiated for any size. A Block Auction must be initiated for at least 10 contracts for a Series and, if applicable, for each Spread leg.

A Responder can submit a response in a Block Auction for any size, but a response will not qualify for a pro-rata allocation (as defined above) in a completed Block auction unless either the size of the response is:

(a) at least 10% of the aggregate size of responders presumptively matched in the completed Block Auction or

(b) less than 10% of the aggregate size of responders that would permit presumptive matching, but including the undersized response would allow for a presumptive match.

Example for (b):​

Assume an initiating order for 100 contracts – with a size condition of All or None – with responses matching on all other terms for (1) 55 contracts, (2) 40 contracts, and (3) 5 contracts. In this example, the 10% limitation is set at 10 contracts. Although the (3) 5 contract response is below the 10% limit, including (3) will result in an aggregate of 100 contracts and permit a presumptive matching against the All or None 100 contracts in the initiating order.

Auction Parameters​

Block Auctions​

Auction Strategy​

SpiderRock currently offers a total of five execution strategies, two of which are auctions (Block Auction, Flash Auction), and three are electronic algorithms (Spider Sweep, Active Maker, Spider TWAP).

Size Condition​

The user can choose between three size conditions:

• All Or None - When selected, a trade may only occur with a response that matches the initiator’s full size.


• Up To Quantity – When selected, a trade may occur with any response for a size up to or equal to the initiator’s full size.


• Quantity Or More – When selected, a trade may occur with a response that has a size greater than the initiator’s displayed size.

Time Duration​

Each Block Auction has a set time determined by the initiating client. The initiating client can choose from this list of auction durations:

• 15 Seconds


• 30 Seconds


• 60 Seconds


• 2 Minutes


• 5 Minutes

This time selection acts as the ultimate duration of the auction and encourages participants to negotiate price levels accordingly. All Block Auctions can be executed immediately at the Public Price which may result in the auction terminating before the Auction Time has elapsed. The time remaining on each auction is displayed on the order cards, the auction cards, as well as on the order ticket itself.

Counterparties​

The counterparty menu allows the initiator to customize the recipients of their auction notices and is a key value-added feature of the Block Auction. The initiator may choose any combination of the below from the counterparty menu:

• SR Network – This is comprised of all clients of SpiderRock Connect. By choosing the SR Network, the initiator can choose to anonymously broadcast their auction notice to all SpiderRock Connect clients OR to disclose their identity.


• Direct Counterparties – These are the established trading relationships of the initiator. The initiator can choose to send their auction to any counterparty anonymously or select the Disclose toggle to reveal their identity to that counterparty. Direct counterparties are organized into two distinct groups:


    o Paid Counterparties – These are firms that have an existing trading relationship with the initiating client where a signed trading agreement between the two firms exists. When the initiating client chooses to trade with this type of counterparty, they will be paying commission directly to the counterparty based on their prearranged agreement. These counterparties are differentiated on the application by using a yellow $ icon. Note: Paid counterparties have to be set to Disclose.


    o Unpaid Counterparties – These are firms that have a relationship with the initiating customer but do not have a signed trading agreement. Both the initiating client and the responding liquidity provider pay commission to SpiderRock Connect on successful auctions.

Public Price​

Public Price is the user’s limit price, which is visible to all participants. The user can choose to update their Public Price at any point during the auction duration, making the auction process interactive for both the initiating and responding clients.

SpiderRock provides price guidance by automatically populating the Public Price field with our fair value calculation. The user can choose to override this value at any time with a price of their own.

Users can also choose to start a one-sided RFQ in which SpiderRock notifies responders of their direction and size, but not price.

Best Price Offset​

The initiator has the option to enter a Best Price Offset, which is a hidden ultimate limit (represented as an offset to the Public Price). For initiators, this Best Price Offset will only be used at the completion of the auction time.

If there is no match of Public Prices of initiating and responding clients during the set auction time, the Best Price of the initiator may be matched with the Public Price or Best Price of a responding client (the Best Price of a responding client may also be matched with the Public Price of the initiating client).

For auction responders, the Best Price Offset will automatically update the responder’s Public Price as other responders show responses that are more aggressive. This is under the assumption that the more aggressive responses are within the responder’s Best Price Offset bound. For example, if the responder entered a Public Price of 1.49withaBestPriceOffsetof1.49 with a Best Price Offset of 1.49withaBestPriceOffsetof0.01, they are indicating that they are willing to update their Public Price to 1.48ifanotherresponder(s)entersaPublicPriceof1.48 if another responder(s) enters a Public Price of 1.48ifanotherresponder(s)entersaPublicPriceof1.48 for the full auction size.

    Note: A responder’s Best Price Offset will only be used to match a more aggressive responder’s price that does not result in an immediate trade.

If the stock moves to 99.90,thebidpricewillbeadjustedto99.90, the bid price will be adjusted to 99.90,thebidpricewillbeadjustedto0.95.

(2-way)		This method is used to adjust limit prices on outright option transactions (without a stock leg), as well as option packages that include a stock leg. Delta (2-way) will apply a delta/gamma adjustment if the stock moves higher or the stock moves lower. For packages that include a stock leg Delta (2-way) will allow the user to adjust on delta that is different from the stock ratio used on the package. For single options or packages that are outright (no stock leg), SpiderRock will adjust the user’s Public Price and Best Price Offset either higher or lower on the user’s defined delta as the underlying stock moves.

Flash Auctions​

Flash Auctions are always IOC orders, and given their short duration, they are only shown to electronic responders capable of responding in 100ms or less. The immediate nature of these auctions also means there is far less customization on their parameters (shown below). While initiating customers can select Flash Auction as a strategy from the strategy pulldown menu on our parameters, Flash Auction responders need to be integrated with SpiderRock via an API such as MLink (SpiderRock’s proprietary streaming API), SRSE (SpiderRock’s SQL-based API), or FIX.

Once an initiator has selected Flash Auction as their trading strategy, they have the ability to enter an order size as well as a limit price. Once the Flash Auction is started, the user is not able to edit the order due to the IOC duration.
